I adopted Roman in San Francisco. There's a weekend every year where one woman pays all the adoption fees for rescue dogs, and I wanted another dog. To be honest, I was there to adopt a different dog, but when I got there, someone had just started adopting him. I was sad, but walked around looking at the other rescues. Roman was sitting on his foster mom's lap, looking sleepy as can be. I said hi, scratched his face, and he gave me a kiss. That was it. I was in love and adopted him right then. He was underweight and his fur on the back of his legs was stained yellow. He likely was caged 24/7 and had to lay in his own pee. He also had cherry eye and underwent surgery right after I took him home. It didn't take, so he got another surgery on that eye. Then he got cherry eye in the other eye, and got it fixed. He'd have cute droopy eyelids for the rest of his life. Between getting fixed and his 3 eye surgeries, he spent the first couple months with me in a cone. Watching him place the cone over the food bowl to eat was so cute. He never got the hang of it and ran into things constantly.

![](/images/roman/img_20150626_151059.jpeg)

He got his name from a character in Grand Theft Auto. His first brother, Niko, was named for the main character, and Roman was his hilarious cousin. Little did I know he'd live up to the name with his quirky and loyal personality.

![](/images/roman/img_20151020_231033.jpeg)

He was my best friend through the lonliest and darkest time of my life. We'd cuddle on the couch, cuddle in bed, and take long walks together. Such a loving dog. Half the time he'd decide he was done and lay down on the sidewalk, usually at the furthest point from home, demanding to be carried home. That lead to a ton of "awww"‘s from strangers as I carried my slightly-too-big-to-be-carried chunky boy until he decided he was ready to walk again. I rolled my eyes but really I didn't mind carrying him.

![](/images/roman/img_1859.jpg)

Roman helped me find the love of my life. He was in my dating profile, and Jenny half-jokes she swiped for Roman more than me. It was love at first sight between her and Roman. One time early in our relationship, I asked her to help me give him a haircut. I accidentally snipped his ear and he started bleeding. I was so worried she'd think I was terrible for hurting him, but she only fell harder seeing how concerned I was about him. After a few months, she adopted Thor and they were best buds almost immediately. Well, after Roman tried humping little 2.5lb baby Thor, which was hilarious to watch. Thor had bad anxiety whenever we'd kennel them and leave, until one day he went in Roman's kennel with him and was content. For the rest of their lives, they were kenneled together, snuggling up when we left. Roman wouldn't let anyone or anything get near his food (probably due to his abuse as a pup), but would patiently watch Thor eat right from his bowl. Roman almost peed on Thor more times than I can count, not waiting until Thor was done peeing to try and mark over him. Even though I adopted him, he was a huge momma's boy as soon as he met Jenny. Somehow even though Roman was "mine" and Thor was "hers", the dogs swapped us and Jenny was Roman's person. Don't worry, I get it buddy.

![](/images/roman/img_1391.jpeg)

![](/images/roman/img_8991.jpeg)

He lived as full a life as any dog. He loved car rides and boy did he get a lot. He drove from San Francisco to Chicgo, Chicago to Los Angeles, Los Angeles to Seattle to Chicago, and then finally to Madison. He went backpacking with me and snuggled in my sleeping bag when it go down to freezing. He loved going to cabins in the middle of nowhere Wisconsin and cabins in the mountains of California. He summited Mount Islip in LA. 200 feet from the summit, he decided he'd had enough and laid down. We had to sit with him for 20 minutes on the side of a mountain before he was ready to finish the hike. He was stubborn and independent like that. He loved the ocean and rivers. He'd bark at them and bite the waves as they came in. We picked our house partially because of a big bay window facing the backyard and woods. He loved it and laid there every chance he could, staring out the window, barking at the squirrels and bunnies. The window was always covered in smudges from his nose. He loved walking the whole fence, sniffing and peeing. One of his greatest moments was chasing down a bunny and standing over it menacingly, unsure of what to do with it. Good job buddy.

![](/images/roman/img_7397.jpeg)

![](/images/roman/img_0477.jpeg)

Jenny and I had so many inside jokes with and about Roman. We gave him a ton of hair styles, dressed him up in ridiculous outfits, trolled him, made weird voices for him, and constantly played games with him. He gave kisses non stop with his big wet tongue. We let him lick a lemon once. He scrunched up his face, licked it again, then grabbed the whole thing and guarded it, even though he clearly hated it. But he wasn't about to let anyone have his "treat". He had a habit of sucking in his upper left lip to let you know he wanted something. His smile while he was panting was the cutest thing in the world. He would wake Jenny up with body slams every morning. He'd wait for her outside the bathroom to go to bed every night. Covid was one of the best things that happened for him. He got to be with his favorite people 24/7 and Jenny would share a ton of food with him, leading to his own version of the "Covid 15" weight gain. If his brother took too long to eat his food, he would sit by the door, wait for us to get up to let him out, then rush over and finish Thor's bowl. He wasn't always the smartest dog, but when it came to obtaining food, he was brilliant. He could do "spin" but only one direction. When you had a treat, he would throw out every trick he knew in random order, whatever it took. He could only pee with his right leg in the air, never the left. He would lick his nose all the time, but only on one side, so we had to put balm on the other side. If he got bored, he'd grab the end of the toilet paper roll and drag it out of the bathroom. There are a hundred more little quirks like that.

![](/images/roman/img_0213-1.jpeg)

When he was diagnosed with congestive heart failure in December, our world was shattered. He was only 8 years old. His doctor told us the left side of his heart was 2.5 times as big as it was supposed to be. That made sense since he was so full of love. The next 8 months would be filled with love, multiple ER visits (including one 2 days before and through our wedding day), one surgery that we hoped would give him months to years of life, and so many tears. We had imagined him being a part of our wedding, but fate had other plans. Luckily he made it out of the ER the day after. We set up our arch in the backyard, put on our wedding clothes, dressed both dogs up in their little tuxes, and had a beautiful private ceremony as a family. I'll cherish that perfect moment forever.

![](/images/roman/img_1149.jpg)

Thankfully, Roman had no idea he was sick. To him, life got better. Mom and dad wanted to spend even more time with him, he got constant car rides, he got better food, and multiple times a day he'd hear an alarm and us belting out "Ohhhhhh Roman!" before giving him a treat with meds.

![](/images/roman/img_1113.jpeg)

I have to give a special thanks to the UW Vet Clinic, especially Dr. Fultz. She helped us so many times, once responding at 11:30pm on a Saturday when all the ERs were full to help us through what to do. She also came back after a long long day at the clinic so we could hand feed him dinner in the ER, because he was a big grump and didn't want to eat from anyone else. She got us in for the surgery that gave us extra weeks with him. His last visit, as soon as I walked him into the ER, multiple techs smiled and said "Hi Roman!". He was (sadly) a little celebrity there with his flowing hair, cute smile, big curly ears, and bigger personality. Thank you to all the vets there that gave him such good care, helping him on days off, and making him comfortable.

![](/images/roman/img_5652.jpeg)

Yesterday, his little body gave out after fighting so hard for so long to get more time with us. We did the hardest thing and called for him to be put to sleep in our backyard. He was afraid of so many things. People stealing his food. Scooters, skateboards, motorcycles, garbage cans, children, really anything loud and moving. Even as he was being sedated, he fought so hard to stay awake and spend the last couple minutes with his mom and dad. I think the thing he was afraid of most was leaving us, knowing how crushed we'd be without him. I'm glad he's not afraid any more.

![](/images/roman/img_1326.jpeg)

I owe him so much and I don't think this hole in my heart will ever go away. Saying goodbye broke my heart. But if this pain is the price we pay for all those years of unconditional love, it's a trade I'd make every single time. We love you and miss you so much Roman.
