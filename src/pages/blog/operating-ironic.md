This post describes a presentation I gave at the OpenStack Vancouver summit. The talk focused on implementing OpenStack Ironic, an early-stage project, in production environments.

## Key Points

I discussed how our team deployed Ironic to power Rackspace's OnMetal offering. Much of the infrastructure tooling we created—particularly driver and decommissioning tools—was released as open source software and eventually integrated into the Ironic project itself.

A significant contribution involved upstreaming decommissioning functionality, which I led personally.

## Summary

The post highlights the practical challenges and achievements of running cutting-edge cloud infrastructure software in production before it reached general availability, demonstrating how early adoption and upstream contributions can drive both innovation and community benefit.
